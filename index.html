    California Wildfire Prediction Platform

    The California Wildfire Prediction project utilizes machine learning to predict wildfire risks in California, aiding real estate investment decisions. Integrating clustering, classification, and regression models with datasets on historical wildfires and meteorological data from Wikipedia and NOAA, the project achieves up to 94.62% accuracy.   • Machine Learning

    NLPoetica: Advanced Poetry Analysis and Generation

    The project was an in-depth exploration into the role of Natural Language Processing (NLP) in the analysis and generation of poetry, with a particular emphasis on the distinctive styles of William Blake and Robert Frost. It encompassed a thorough study, wherein a large corpus of poems was meticulously scraped and analyzed using state-of-the-art computational techniques for linguistic and thematic insights. A notable achievement of the project was the fine-tuning of the Mistral 7b Large Language Model, specifically tailored for poetry generation, which exhibited a high semantic similarity to the original poets' styles. To enhance the model's ability to generate contextually relevant poetry, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) was integrated via Weaviate. Moreover, the project included the development of Knowledge Graphs to associate poetic content with contemporary societal issues, highlighting the utility of NLP in literary analysis. A wide range of technologies, including Python, BeautifulSoup, TensorFlow, Weaviate, NetworkX, and various other NLP tools, were utilized. The project provided significant insights into the intricate interplay between language, creativity, and societal discourse, making a substantial contribution to the field of computational creativity.
